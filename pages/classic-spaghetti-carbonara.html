Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

A creamy and comforting italian dish made with eggs, cheese, pancheta, and pepper. It's rich, savory, and perfect for a quick weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 200 spaghetti
  • 150 g pancheta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves galic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps to Make:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instrucions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta weater, then drain the pasta.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the panchetta and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook for additional 1 minute.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet with panchetta. Toss to combine.
  5. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly mix in the egg mixture, tossing the pasta to coat. Add reserved pasta water as needed to reach desired consitstency.
  6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired, and serve immediately.

Time to cook:

20 minutes

Substitutes:

  • Panchetta can be substituted with bacon.
  • Parmesan can be replaced with Pecorino Romano.
  • For a vegetarian option, omit the panchetta and use mushrooms for a similar umami flavor.
